Program Design & Procurement

01

Hybrid Ownership Model

Flexible approach: city-owned units for field crews with vendor monitoring, plus vendor-managed services for markets, events, and parks. Not one-size-fits-all – designed around your budget and procurement constraints.

02

Performance-Based RFP Support

[PLACEHOLDER: Help staff write RFPs that specify outcomes (uptime, safety, reporting) instead of prescriptive hardware specs. Evaluation criteria include municipal experience, fleet management capabilities, and reliability track record – not just lowest price.]

03

Discovery to Pilot Launch

Process: Stakeholder discovery → Draft program brief → City RFP (if needed) → Vendor selection → Pilot kickoff. Timeline typically 3-6 months depending on procurement requirements and grant approval cycles.

Operations & Fleet Management

Markets: Weekly Power Distribution

[PLACEHOLDER: Vendor receives weekly market schedule. Two operational models: (A) Drop off batteries Friday, pick up Sunday, or (B) Support city check-out system where vendors collect from city facility. Telemetry tracks typical loads per stall type (food prep, refrigeration, lighting).]

Events: On-Demand Deployment

[PLACEHOLDER: Event organizer submits intake form (date, location, expected loads). Vendor proposes battery configuration and quote. Event day: delivery, setup, on-site support during event, teardown. SLA guarantees replacement within 4 hours if unit fails.]

Parks: Rental Integration

[PLACEHOLDER: Park reservation system includes clean power requirement. Bounce house vendor or party renter contacts battery vendor directly (vendor contact provided in permit). Same-day delivery and pickup. Multi-battery configs for 4+ hour blower runtime.]

Fleet Management Software

Real-time dashboard showing battery state of charge, temperature, location, and load. Automated alerts for issues. Maintenance workflows flag weak cells. Grant reporting: kWh delivered, emissions avoided, utilization tracking.
